# Outline: The Locked-Up Lullabies

**Premise:** A traveling puzzle-maker arrives with a beautiful music box locked by seven tiny puzzle-locks that nobody in the land can open — until Savannah and her friends take a crack at it.
**Characters:** Princess Savannah, Luna, Rascal the Raccoon, Hazel the Owl, Big Al
**Setting:** A cozy kingdom village with a cobblestone square, lantern-lit market stalls, and a big old fountain in the middle where everyone gathers on warm evenings

## Beats

1. **The peaceful kingdom.** It's a soft summer evening in the village. Savannah and Luna are in the square, where lanterns glow and critters are settling in for the night. Luna is sprawled across the warm cobblestones, yawning enormous yawns and demanding belly scratches from anyone who walks by. Rascal is up on a market stall roof, poking through someone's wind chimes. Hazel is perched on the fountain, watching the stars come out and asking nobody in particular why the moon is always a different shape. It's the kind of evening where nothing needs to happen and that's perfectly fine.

2. **The problem.** A gentle old puzzle-maker named Wendell arrives in the square, pulling a little wooden cart. On the cart sits the most beautiful music box anyone has ever seen — carved with stars and moons and sleeping animals, polished until it glows. But the box is locked with seven tiny puzzle-locks, each one different, and Wendell can't open it. He's traveled to every village in the kingdom. Clever folk, strong folk, patient folk — nobody has solved all seven. He looks tired and a little sad. He says he built the box himself years ago to hold something precious inside, but the locks were enchanted and now even he can't remember how they work. He just wants to hear what's inside one more time.

3. **The call.** Savannah walks up and asks if she can try. Wendell smiles and says of course — everyone is welcome to try. Luna sniffs the box, rumbles low, and presses her ear against it. Her eyes go wide. She nudges Savannah urgently, like she can hear something faint inside. That settles it. Savannah gathers her friends — Rascal, because he's clever with his paws and finds solutions in everything; Hazel, because she thinks carefully and asks the right questions; and she goes to find Big Al, who lives in the stone workshop at the edge of the square. Big Al turns the box over in his huge hands, studies it, and says, "Seven locks. You don't need to be the smartest — you just need to be patient enough to listen to each one." He settles down on a bench to watch.

4. **The adventure.** Savannah and her friends work through the seven locks one at a time, each one a different kind of small puzzle. The first is a sliding tile — Rascal's quick paws rearrange the pieces while Savannah tells him which picture they're making (a crescent moon). The second is a pattern of stars that need to be pressed in the right order — Hazel studies them, tilts her head, and figures out they match a constellation she watches every night. The third is a riddle etched into the brass: "I have no voice but I can make you sleep. I have no arms but I hold you." Savannah thinks quietly, then whispers, "A lullaby." Click. The fourth lock has a tiny keyhole shaped like a dragon — Luna presses her claw in and it fits perfectly, which delights her enormously. She prances. The fifth is a knot of silver thread that has to be untangled — Rascal and Savannah work it together, patient and slow. The sixth is a lock that only opens when you hum the right note — Hazel hums low, Savannah hums high, and Luna lets out a rumbling purr that hits the exact right tone. Six locks open. But the seventh lock is blank. No puzzle, no riddle, no keyhole. Just smooth, warm wood. They all stare at it. Wendell watches from his cart, quiet.

5. **The twist.** They try everything — pushing, tapping, whispering to it. Nothing works. Savannah sits back, thinking. Big Al leans over and says gently, "Some locks don't need solving. They need asking." Savannah places her hand flat on the seventh lock and says, simply, "Please." The lock clicks open. It was never a puzzle at all — it just needed someone kind enough to ask. Wendell's eyes fill with tears, and he says that's the one lock nobody ever tried. Everyone was so busy being clever, nobody thought to just be gentle.

6. **The resolution.** The music box opens slowly, and from inside rises the most beautiful lullaby anyone has ever heard — soft and warm and golden, like someone humming you to sleep on a summer night. It floats out over the square and through the whole village. The critters curl up where they are. Rascal tucks his tail over his nose. Hazel's eyes drift shut on the fountain. Luna lays her big head in Savannah's lap and sighs. Even Big Al leans back on his bench and closes his eyes with a small smile. Wendell listens with his hands clasped, finally hearing his lullaby again after all this time. The whole kingdom yawns and smiles.

7. **Happily ever after.** Wendell gives the music box to Savannah — he says it belongs with someone who knows how to listen. Every night after that, Savannah opens the box at bedtime, and the lullaby drifts out over the village, and everyone sleeps a little deeper and dreams a little sweeter. Luna always insists on being the one to press the dragon-shaped lock, just because she can. And if you listen very carefully on a warm summer evening, you can still hear it playing — soft and golden, all the way to the edge of the kingdom.
