# Outline: The Song the Kingdom Forgot

**Premise:** It is the morning before the Midsummer Concert, and no one in the kingdom can remember the Midsummer Song — not even Big Al. Savannah sets out with Luna and her friends to find the missing pieces of the song before sundown.
**Characters:** Princess Savannah, Luna, Big Al, Hazel the Owl, Mossy the Turtle, Cricket the Cricket (new)
**Setting:** A warm green kingdom on midsummer morning. Meadows full of wildflowers, a great stage draped in garlands at the heart of the village, and a winding path that leads out through the Old Hollow, along the Slow River, and up into the Clover Hills.

## Beats

1. **The peaceful kingdom.** It is the longest day of the year — Midsummer — and the whole kingdom is preparing for the Midsummer Concert. Garlands are being strung, instruments are being tuned, the stage is set. Every year the kingdom sings the Midsummer Song together at sunset. It is the song that everyone knows by heart. Except this morning, nobody can remember it. Big Al stands by the stage, humming and frowning. He can remember the feeling of the song — warm and bright — but not the notes. Nobody else can remember it either. It is as if the song simply slipped away in the night.

2. **The problem.** Savannah finds Big Al looking troubled. He explains that the Midsummer Song is made of four pieces — the melody, the rhythm, the harmony, and the hum that holds it all together. Without all four, there is no song. And somehow, every single piece has gone missing. The concert is at sunset. The kingdom needs its song back.

3. **The call.** Savannah decides to go find the missing pieces. Luna is right beside her, rumbling and ready. Hazel the Owl swoops down and says she knows where old songs go when they are lost — out into the world, to the places that remember sound. Big Al tells Savannah he believes in her and sends them on their way.

4. **Visit 1: The Old Hollow.** Savannah, Luna, and Hazel travel to the Old Hollow — a great ancient tree so wide you can walk inside it. Hazel listens carefully to the echo inside the trunk. She hears it — a thread of the melody, caught in the rings of the old wood, humming faintly. Hazel coaxes it out by asking exactly the right question. The melody drifts out like a golden ribbon. Savannah catches it gently. **"One piece found."**

5. **Visit 2: The Slow River.** They follow the path to the Slow River, where Mossy the Turtle sits on his favorite rock, watching the water go by. Savannah tells him what they are looking for. Mossy listens to the river for a long, patient while. Then he nods. The rhythm is there — in the steady lap of the water against the stones, the patient pulse the river has been keeping all along. Mossy taps it out on his shell. The rhythm rises up like a warm drumbeat. **"Two pieces found."**

6. **Visit 3: The Clover Hills.** They climb up into the Clover Hills, where the grass is tall and humming with insects. There they meet Cricket the Cricket — small, bright-voiced, and full of enthusiasm. Cricket has been singing nonstop all morning and doesn't know why. It turns out Cricket has the harmony — it has been living in his song all day. He chirps it out, bright and clear, and the harmony joins the melody and rhythm like a missing puzzle piece clicking into place. **"Three pieces found."**

7. **The twist.** Three pieces found — but there are four. The hum that holds it all together is still missing. Savannah worries. They search the hills. They listen to the wind. Nothing. Then Hazel tilts her head and says, "Wait. Listen." And they all go quiet. And there it is — a deep, steady, rumbling hum. It is Luna. Luna has been humming the whole journey, a low, warm rumble in her chest, the way she always does when she is happy and close to Savannah. She has been carrying the fourth piece the entire time without knowing it.

8. **The resolution.** They race back to the village with all four pieces. Big Al is waiting. Savannah gives him the melody, the rhythm, the harmony — and Luna steps forward and gives the hum. Big Al puts his hands together and the song comes flooding back, whole and warm and bright. The entire kingdom remembers it at once. The concert begins at sunset, and the Midsummer Song rings out across the meadow, with Luna's deep rumble holding it all together from underneath.

9. **Happily ever after.** The kingdom sings together in the golden evening light. Savannah stands between her friends, Luna humming beside her, Hazel on a branch overhead, Mossy tapping along on his shell, Cricket chirping the harmony from the tall grass. Big Al's deep voice carries over them all. The song was never really lost — it was just spread out among friends, waiting to be gathered back together. And from that Midsummer on, whenever someone forgot the song, they only had to listen to Luna's rumble to remember how it began.

## Repeating Motif

"One piece found... two pieces found... three pieces found..."
