# Outline: The Mixed-Up Map

**Premise:** Big Al finds a torn-up treasure map in his attic, and Savannah and her friends race across the kingdom to collect and decode the scattered pieces — only to discover the "treasure" is the perfect hilltop for watching stars together.
**Characters:** Princess Savannah, Luna, Big Al, Rascal the Raccoon, Bramble the Bat
**Setting:** A cozy kingdom with rolling green hills, old stone bridges, a village with a bell tower, and deep friendly woods full of hollow oaks and winding paths. The kind of place where every corner has a story tucked into it.

## Beats

1. **The peaceful kingdom.** It's a lazy golden afternoon in the kingdom. Savannah and Luna are sprawled on the front steps of Big Al's cottage, bored in the best possible way — the kind of bored where you've already chased butterflies, already splashed in the creek, and now you're just lying there watching clouds. Luna keeps sighing dramatically, flopping from one side to the other, because she is a dragon who needs everyone to know when she is understimulated. Big Al is inside, rummaging around in his attic. He's been up there all morning. They can hear thumps and clatters and the occasional "well, I'll be." It's warm and safe and slow.

2. **The problem.** Big Al comes downstairs holding a single torn piece of old parchment — yellowed, curled at the edges, with a bit of a drawing on it and some faded writing. He says he found it wedged behind an old trunk, and he's sure it's part of a treasure map he made a long, long time ago. But the map has been torn into three pieces, and only one was in the attic. He remembers now — years ago, he hid the pieces in three different spots across the kingdom so the map itself would be an adventure to find. The trouble is, his memory is a little fuzzy on the details. He remembers a hollow oak, the old stone bridge, and the bell tower — but not exactly where in each spot he tucked the pieces. And each piece, he says with a wink, has a little riddle on it that has to be solved before the next piece makes sense.

3. **The call.** Savannah is on her feet before Big Al even finishes talking. A treasure map? With riddles? Luna is already at the door, tail swishing, letting out a low, bossy rumble that clearly means "we're going NOW." Big Al laughs and tells them to find Rascal and Bramble — they'll need Rascal's clever paws for finding hidden things, and Bramble's sharp eyes for scouting in dark places. Savannah rounds up the crew. Rascal is thrilled (he lives for finding things). Bramble is quiet but ready, already thinking three steps ahead. Big Al hands Savannah the first piece and tells her the riddle on it points to where the second piece is hidden. Off they go.

4. **The adventure.** Three mini-quests, each with its own rhythm:
   - **The Hollow Oak.** The first riddle leads them to the oldest oak in the woods, the one with a trunk so wide four people could hide inside it. But the piece isn't just sitting there — it's tucked up high in a crook where the branches split. Rascal scrambles up in seconds, nimble as anything, and finds it wedged under a knot of bark. He's so pleased with himself he takes a little bow from the branch. The second piece has another riddle and a piece of the map showing a winding river.
   - **The Stone Bridge.** The second riddle points to the old stone bridge over Tumble Creek. They search the top, the sides, the railings — nothing. Bramble suggests looking underneath. She flies below the bridge, scanning the dark mossy stones, and spots a tiny rolled-up scroll tucked into a crack between two stones. Luna tries to help by sticking her big dragon head under the bridge, which mostly just blocks all the light and gets her horns stuck for a moment. Everyone has to help wiggle her free. (Luna pretends this was on purpose.) The third piece has the final riddle and shows a hilltop with stars drawn above it.
   - **The Bell Tower.** The last riddle leads to the village bell tower. It's tall and echoey inside, with a winding staircase. Savannah climbs the stairs with her friends. At the top, the final piece is tucked behind the big brass bell. Rascal spots it, Savannah reaches it, and when she pulls it free the bell gives one low, deep bong that echoes across the whole kingdom. Luna answers it with a triumphant roar. Now they have all three pieces.

5. **The twist.** They spread the three pieces out on the grass and fit them together. The full map shows a path leading to a hilltop on the far side of the kingdom, marked with a big X and the words "the treasure." They race to the spot, expecting — well, they're not sure. Gold? Gems? A magic something? But when they get to the top of the hill, there's nothing there. Just grass, wildflowers, and the widest, most open view of the sky they've ever seen. Savannah is confused for a moment. Then Big Al arrives, walking up the hill behind them with a blanket over one arm and a lantern in his hand. He smiles and says, "You found it." The treasure isn't something buried. It's this spot — the perfect hill for watching the stars come out. He marked it on a map years ago so he'd never forget it.

6. **The resolution.** Big Al spreads the blanket out on the hilltop. Everyone settles in as the sky turns from gold to pink to deep, deep blue. Rascal curls up and points out shapes in the first stars. Bramble, who knows the night sky better than anyone, names the constellations in her quiet voice. Luna stretches out long across the blanket, warm as a furnace, and everyone leans against her like she's the world's best dragon-shaped couch. Savannah sits next to Big Al and tells him this is the best treasure she's ever found. Big Al says the real adventure was watching her find it.

7. **Happily ever after.** The stars fill the sky, more and more, until the whole hilltop glows with silver light. Luna hums a low, rumbly dragon-purr. Rascal's eyes are already half-closed. Bramble spreads her wings once, softly, then tucks them in. Savannah leans against Big Al's arm and watches one bright star wink at her from the very top of the sky. And if you ever find that hill on a clear night, they say you can still see them there — a princess, a dragon, a raccoon, a bat, and a big, strong man with a warm heart — watching the stars together. And they lived happily ever after.
