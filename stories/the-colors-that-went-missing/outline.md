# Outline: The Colors That Went Missing

**Premise:** Savannah wakes to find every color in the kingdom has faded to gray — and follows a trail of paint drips into the forest to discover a shy little chameleon who accidentally soaked up all the color because she was too afraid to show her own.
**Characters:** Princess Savannah, Luna, Wilma, Clover the Deer, Petal the Chameleon (new critter — tiny, shy, sweet, changes color with her feelings but has been trying so hard to hide that she pulled all the color inward)
**Setting:** Savannah's cozy kingdom in early morning — the castle, the meadow, the winding forest path, and a quiet mossy hollow deep in the woods where Petal has been hiding.

## Beats

1. **The peaceful kingdom.** It's the very first moment of morning, and Princess Savannah is up before anyone else. She loves to sit at the tall window in her tower room and watch the sunrise paint the sky — pink, then gold, then blue. Luna is curled at the foot of her bed, snoring softly (and loudly), her black scales shimmering in the early light. Savannah has her paints out and ready. She's going to paint the sunrise today. Everything is warm and still and perfect.

2. **The problem.** But when Savannah looks out the window, the sunrise isn't there. The sky is white — not cloudy-white, just... empty-white. She looks down at the meadow. The wildflowers are gray. The trees are gray. Even the river looks pale and thin. She turns to Luna, and Luna's beautiful black scales have gone dull and ashy, like charcoal left out in the rain. Luna wakes up, shakes herself, looks at her own paws, and lets out a confused, offended bark — because Luna always has opinions, and "gray" is not one she approves of. Savannah checks her paint pots. Every single color has faded to nothing. The whole kingdom has gone gray overnight.

3. **The call.** Savannah isn't frightened — she's determined. Colors don't just disappear. They have to go somewhere. She kneels down and spots something on her windowsill: a tiny drip of blue, bright as a berry. Then another on the stone stairs — a drop of gold. Then a smudge of pink on the garden gate. A trail. Savannah grabs her empty paint satchel, calls Luna to her side, and follows the color drips toward the forest. Luna trots beside her, sniffing each bright drop and sneezing dramatically at the pink ones.

4. **The adventure.** The trail leads into the deep woods, where even the shadows have gone pale. Along the way, they find Clover the Deer standing very still among the gray trees, her usually warm brown coat turned silver. Clover doesn't say much — she never does — but she falls into step beside Savannah, quiet and steady. The three of them follow the drips deeper in. The trail gets brighter as they go — more drops, closer together, richer colors — like whatever took the color is leaving it behind little by little. They come to Wilma's cottage at a bend in the path. Wilma is standing in her garden, calm as always, her silver hair the one thing that looks right in a gray world. She tells Savannah she felt the colors slip away in the night, like a song fading out. She says the colors weren't taken with meanness — they were taken with loneliness. She gives Savannah a small, empty glass jar and says: "When you find where the colors went, you won't need to take them back. You'll just need to help them feel safe enough to come out." Savannah tucks the jar into her satchel and presses on.

5. **The twist.** The trail ends at a mossy hollow beneath an old oak tree. And there, curled into the tightest, smallest ball, is a tiny chameleon — no bigger than Savannah's hand. But she's not the dull green of an ordinary chameleon. She is blazing with color. Every color that's missing from the kingdom is swirling across her skin — pinks and golds and blues and deep forest greens and sunset oranges — shifting and rippling like she can't contain it all. She's trembling. She's scared. When she sees Savannah, she tries to squeeze herself smaller, and as she does, the colors pulse brighter, like they're trying to burst out of her. This is Petal. She didn't steal the colors on purpose. She's a chameleon who changes color with her feelings, and she's been so afraid of being seen — so afraid of standing out — that she curled up tight and tried to disappear. But the harder she tried to hide, the more color she pulled in from everything around her. She took the whole kingdom's color trying to make herself invisible.

6. **The resolution.** Savannah doesn't scold her. She doesn't grab the colors back. She sits down right there in the moss, cross-legged, and speaks softly. She tells Petal that her colors are beautiful. Luna, never one for subtlety, flops down beside Petal and nuzzles her with a big scaly nose, as if to say, "You're fine. Stop worrying." Clover lies down nearby, calm and patient, just being there the way Clover always is. Savannah asks Petal if she'd like to come out and see the kingdom — not to give the colors back, just to see it. Slowly, carefully, Petal uncurls. Savannah holds out her hand, and Petal climbs on. And as Petal uncurls — as she stops trying to squeeze herself invisible — the colors begin to drift off her skin like dandelion seeds. Pinks float up to the sky. Greens drift down to the grass. Gold settles into the sunlight. The colors return to where they belong, gently, one by one, not because anyone forced them out, but because Petal isn't trying to hide anymore. By the time they walk back through the forest, the world is blooming with color again. The wildflowers glow. The river shines. Luna's black scales are rich and dark and gleaming, and Luna prances about looking extremely pleased with herself.

7. **Happily ever after.** Back at the kingdom, the sunrise finally arrives — late, but so bright and so pink and so gold that everyone stops to stare. Wilma is waiting at the edge of the meadow, smiling like she knew how this would end. Savannah holds Petal up to the light, and Petal — shy, sweet, tiny Petal — lets her colors shift and shimmer freely for the first time: rose, then violet, then a soft, happy green. She doesn't hide. Savannah sets her on Luna's broad nose, where Petal decides she quite likes the view. Luna goes cross-eyed looking at her and sneezes, which makes everyone laugh. Savannah paints the sunrise after all — and this time, she has every color she could ever need. And Petal, the smallest, shyest creature in the kingdom, never disappears again. She doesn't need to. She has friends now, and friends make it safe to be seen.
