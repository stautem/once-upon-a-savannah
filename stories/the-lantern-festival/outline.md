# Outline: The Lantern Festival

**Premise:** Every autumn the kingdom holds a Lantern Festival where each family floats a paper lantern on the lake at sunset — but Savannah's lantern turns out more beautiful than she ever planned, thanks to Luna's lovable chaos.
**Characters:** Princess Savannah, Luna, Finnegan the Fox, Hazel the Owl, Wilma
**Setting:** A cozy lakeside kingdom in autumn. Golden leaves, warm breezes, a wide still lake that mirrors the sunset sky. The village square is strung with ribbons and bunting. Families gather at long wooden tables to craft their lanterns together, and at dusk the whole kingdom walks down to the water's edge to set them floating.

## Beats

1. **The peaceful kingdom.** It is the first golden day of autumn, and the whole kingdom is buzzing with excitement — tonight is the Lantern Festival. We see Savannah in the village square, surrounded by jars of paint, sheets of colored tissue paper, pots of glue, and bowls of glitter. Finnegan the Fox is there too, carefully cutting perfect star shapes (he is very proud of his sharp scissors work). Hazel the Owl perches on a fence post nearby, watching everything with calm interest and asking questions like, "Do you think the lake remembers last year's lanterns?" The air smells like cinnamon and woodsmoke. Everyone in the kingdom is making a lantern to float on the lake at sunset. Savannah has a plan for hers — she wants it to be perfect.

2. **The problem.** Luna wants to help. She wants to help *so much*. She nudges the paint jars with her big snout and knocks blue paint across the table. She sits on the tissue paper and crinkles it flat. She sniffs the glitter and sneezes an enormous dragon-sneeze that sends glitter flying everywhere — into Savannah's hair, across Finnegan's star shapes, over Hazel's feathers. Every time Savannah starts fresh, Luna finds a new way to make a beautiful mess of things. Finnegan tries to distract Luna with jokes. Hazel suggests Luna count the clouds for a while. Nothing works. Luna just wants to be close to Savannah and be part of the making. By the time the sun starts to dip, Savannah's lantern is lopsided, paint-smeared, glitter-crusted, and patched together with extra glue. It looks nothing like she planned. Savannah is quiet. She isn't angry — but she is a little sad.

3. **The call.** Savannah sits by the lake's edge, holding her messy lantern, watching other families carry their neat, beautiful lanterns down to the water. Finnegan sits beside her and says something like, "It's got character, Savannah. That's better than perfect." Hazel floats down and adds, "The lake doesn't judge lanterns. It just holds them." They are kind, but Savannah still feels the gap between what she imagined and what she has. Then she looks at Luna, who is sitting a little way off, head low, tail still — Luna can tell Savannah is disappointed, and now Luna feels bad too. That is what makes Savannah stand up. She is not going to let her friend feel sad on festival night. She decides they are all going to the lake together, messy lantern and all.

4. **The adventure.** The walk to the lake at dusk. The whole kingdom is heading down the hill in a warm golden procession — families, critters, lanterns glowing softly in the fading light. Savannah carries her lumpy lantern. Luna walks close beside her, still quiet. Finnegan tells jokes to fill the silence ("What did the lantern say to the lake? I'm *glowing* to meet you!"). Hazel flies ahead and reports back on how beautiful the lake looks — perfectly still, like a mirror. They reach the water's edge and line up with everyone else. Wilma is there, standing tall and lovely by the shore, her own lantern simple and elegant. She kneels beside Savannah and looks at the messy lantern without a word of criticism. She runs a gentle hand over the crinkled paper and says something like, "This one was made with a lot of love. You can always tell." She helps Savannah set a small candle inside and shows her how to ease it out onto the water.

5. **The twist.** Savannah sets her lantern on the lake and gives it a gentle push. It wobbles out onto the still water among all the other lanterns — neat round ones, tall ones, ones shaped like stars and moons. Hers looks like a crumpled, glittery mess among them. But then, as the last sliver of sun drops below the hills, something happens. Savannah's lantern begins to glow — not the soft flicker of a candle, but a warm, steady, golden light from deep inside. It glows brighter than any other lantern on the lake. The whole kingdom notices. The light catches all the glitter and scattered paint and makes the lantern shimmer like a jewel. It turns out that while Savannah wasn't looking, Luna breathed one tiny, gentle puff of dragon-fire into the lantern — just a whisper of flame, the smallest gift she knew how to give. She couldn't help with scissors or paint. But she could give it this.

6. **The resolution.** Savannah turns to Luna, and Luna is watching the glowing lantern with big, proud eyes. Savannah throws her arms around Luna's neck and holds on tight. "You were helping the whole time," she whispers. Finnegan grins and says, "I *knew* that lantern had something extra." Hazel nods slowly and says, "The best things are never the ones you planned." The other families gather around to admire the brightest lantern on the lake, and Savannah tells them all — "Luna made it glow." Luna lifts her head high, loud and proud, and lets out a happy rumble that echoes across the water.

7. **Happily ever after.** The kingdom sits together on the shore and watches all the lanterns drift across the dark lake, Savannah's golden one shining at the center like a little sun. Savannah leans against Luna, Finnegan curls up nearby, and Hazel settles on a low branch overhead. Wilma drapes a warm blanket over Savannah's shoulders and says, "Same time next year." The lanterns float on and on, and the brightest one — the messy, lopsided, glitter-covered, dragon-lit one — glows the longest of all. And every autumn after that, when the Lantern Festival comes around, Savannah never makes her lantern alone. She always makes it with Luna. And it is always, always the brightest one on the lake.
