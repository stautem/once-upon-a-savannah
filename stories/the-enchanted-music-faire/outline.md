# The Enchanted Music Faire — Outline

## Premise
A grand music festival with an Enchanted Stage that turns music into colored light. The headline performer is terrified of her grand finale solo, and Savannah helps her find courage.

## Characters
- **Princess Savannah** — the hero, kind and brave
- **Luna** — black dragon, loud and supportive, loves music (badly)
- **Finnegan the Fox** — festival organizer, clever and playful
- **Pepper the Skunk** — backstage helper, sweet-natured and surprisingly brave
- **Velvet the Fox** — headline crooner, Elvis-energy, jeweled cape, deep voice, flamboyant but kind
- **Crescendo the Cricket** — brilliant fiddler, tiny, terrified of playing the grand finale solo
- **The Lily Pads** — a band of singing frogs, green/blue harmonies

## Story Beats

1. **The peaceful kingdom** — The Enchanted Music Faire arrives in the meadow. The stage is magical — it turns music into colored light. Everyone is excited. Savannah, Luna, Finnegan (organizing), and Pepper (backstage) are all there.

2. **The performances begin** — Velvet the Fox takes the stage in his jeweled cape and deep crooning voice. Red and purple light swirls from the stage. The Lily Pads sing next — green and blue harmonies ripple through the air. The crowd is delighted.

3. **The problem** — The grand finale belongs to Crescendo the Cricket and her golden fiddle. But backstage, Savannah and Pepper find Crescendo hiding, terrified. She's afraid the crowd will laugh because she's so small.

4. **The call** — Savannah sits with Crescendo. She listens. She doesn't rush. She tells Crescendo that the music is what matters, not the size of the musician.

5. **The rally** — Savannah gathers Velvet, Luna, Pepper, and the Lily Pads. They each offer encouragement. Luna offers to roar if anyone laughs (nobody will). Velvet says the stage has never cared about size. The Lily Pads offer to hum along so Crescendo won't feel alone.

6. **The twist/resolution** — Crescendo steps onto the enormous stage, tiny and trembling. She lifts her fiddle. The first note rings out — and golden light pours from the stage, brighter than any color that night. As she plays, her golden light weaves together with the red, purple, green, and blue left behind by the other performers. It becomes the biggest, most beautiful magic of the whole faire.

7. **Happily ever after** — The crowd cheers. Crescendo takes a bow. The meadow glows with woven light. Everyone is together, warm, and happy.

## Key Themes
- You don't have to be big to shine bright
- Friends help you find your courage
- The magic was in the music all along
