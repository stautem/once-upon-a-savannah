# Outline: The Dragon Who Loved Jigsaw Puzzles

**Premise:** Luna discovers an ancient stone jigsaw puzzle on the lakeshore and becomes obsessed with solving it — and when the whole kingdom pitches in to finish it, the completed mosaic turns out to be a portrait of a dragon who looks just like her.
**Characters:** Princess Savannah, Luna, Ollie the Opossum, Mossy the Turtle, Clover the Deer
**Setting:** A quiet lakeside kingdom with smooth pebble shores, wildflower meadows, and a big open village green where everyone gathers

## Beats

1. **The peaceful kingdom.** Savannah and Luna spend their mornings by the lake. Luna likes to dig in the shallows and sniff every rock on the shore. Savannah skips stones. Ollie naps in a pile of leaves nearby, Mossy watches the clouds from his favorite warm rock, and Clover comes down to the water's edge to drink, quiet and graceful. It's the kind of morning where nothing needs to happen and that's exactly right.

2. **The problem.** After a big rainstorm, the lake pulls back and uncovers something buried in the mud along the shore — hundreds of flat stone pieces, all different shapes, covered in faded colors. Luna finds them first. She sniffs one, then another, then starts nudging them around with her nose. By the time Savannah arrives, Luna is fully obsessed. She's arranged a dozen pieces into a cluster and is growling softly at the pile, tail thumping, completely locked in. She won't leave. She won't eat. She won't even roar at the squirrels. Savannah has never seen her like this. And the puzzle is enormous — far too many pieces for one dragon, even a very determined one.

3. **The call.** Savannah tries to help, but when she places a piece where Luna doesn't think it goes, Luna gives her a deep, rumbling growl and nudges it back out. Ollie tries too — Luna huffs at him so hard he plays dead for ten minutes. Mossy suggests they slow down and look at the shapes more carefully. "She's not being mean," Mossy says. "She just knows where things go. She can see it." Savannah realizes Luna can't do this alone, but she also can't do it with people placing pieces wrong. So Savannah decides to organize: she'll host a big puzzle party on the village green, move all the pieces there, sort them by color and edge, and let Luna be the boss of where everything goes.

4. **The adventure.** Savannah and her friends carry the stone pieces in baskets up to the village green. It takes all day. Ollie dramatically faints twice under the weight of a single piece ("It's SO heavy, Savannah, I can't POSSIBLY—" and then he carries three more). Mossy is slow but never drops one. Clover appears silently with a basket balanced on her back, sets it down, and disappears to get another without a word. The whole kingdom comes to help sort — pieces spread across the green like a giant colorful carpet. Luna paces around the edges, inspecting everything, rumbling approval or disapproval. Savannah sets up a system: sorters find pieces that might match, hold them up for Luna, and Luna either nods and places the piece herself or snorts and sends them back to try again. It becomes a game. The whole village gets into it — cheering when a piece fits, groaning playfully when Luna rejects one. The mosaic grows, slowly, over the course of a long golden afternoon. An image starts to appear — scales, wings, a proud head — but nobody can quite tell what it is yet.

5. **The twist.** As the picture takes shape, Mossy — who has been studying the stones quietly — notices something. "These stones are very, very old," he says. "Older than the village." Clover finds a piece near the edge with tiny carved words, worn almost smooth. Savannah reads them aloud: "For the first friend." The last piece is placed by Luna herself — she carries it gently in her mouth and sets it right in the center. The mosaic clicks complete, and everyone steps back. It's a dragon. Black-scaled, bright-eyed, wings spread wide, with a look on its face that's brave and gentle all at once. It looks exactly like Luna. The village goes quiet. This mosaic was made long, long ago — a monument to the very first dragon who ever befriended the kingdom. And she looked just like Luna.

6. **The resolution.** Luna stares at the mosaic for a long time. Then she lies down right next to it and rests her big head against the stone dragon's nose. She makes that deep, low rumble — the one that means she's perfectly, completely happy. Savannah sits beside her. Ollie curls up against Luna's side. Clover settles in the grass nearby. Mossy says what everyone is thinking: "She knew. The whole time, she knew it was hers." Savannah and the village decide to keep the mosaic right there on the green, where everyone can see it — the kingdom's oldest treasure, found by its newest dragon.

7. **Happily ever after.** That evening, the whole village sits around the mosaic under the first stars. Luna is asleep in the middle of it, sprawled across the ancient stone portrait like it's the most comfortable bed in the world. Savannah leans against her, warm and close. The fireflies come out over the lake. And if you visit that little kingdom today, you can still see the mosaic on the village green — and a black dragon sleeping on top of it, right where she belongs.
