# Outline: The Garden That Grew Backwards

**Premise:** Savannah's garden is growing in reverse — flowers shrinking, vegetables disappearing back into the soil. She goes underground with Digby, Pip, and Luna to find out why, and discovers a lonely mole cricket named Rosie who was pulling roots down to make her own garden underground. They build a half-underground garden together, shared between both gardeners.
**Characters:** Princess Savannah, Luna the Dragon, Digby the Mole, Pip the Jumping Spider, Rosie the Mole Cricket (new)
**Setting:** A sunlit cottage garden at the edge of a meadow, with a secret world of tunnels and caverns just below the surface

## Beats

1. **The peaceful kingdom.** Savannah tends her garden every morning with the same little ritual: "Good morning, garden. Good morning, sun. Good morning, everyone." Luna dozes in the warm dirt between the rows. Digby lives in a cozy burrow at the garden's edge. Pip bounces along the leaves, inspecting dewdrops. Everything is growing tall and bright.

2. **The problem.** One morning, the sunflowers are shorter than yesterday. The next day, the carrots have sunk deeper into the soil. The tomatoes shrink from red to green to tiny buds. The whole garden is growing backwards — undoing itself, day by day. Savannah says her morning greeting and the garden doesn't seem to answer back the way it used to.

3. **The call.** Savannah decides to find out what's happening. Digby notices the roots are being pulled downward, not wilting — something underground is tugging them. He knows the tunnels and offers to lead the way. Pip wants to come because she spots tiny claw marks on the roots that nobody else can see. Luna insists on coming too, even though she is much too big for tunnels.

4. **The adventure.** They go underground. Digby leads them through winding tunnels. "Deeper and deeper, darker and warmer, closer and closer." Pip rides on Savannah's shoulder and spots clues — thread-thin root trails, tiny scratches in the tunnel walls, the faintest glow ahead. Luna squeezes through, grumbling and getting stuck, but refuses to be left behind. The tunnels open into wider caverns lit by glowing mushrooms and underground streams.

5. **The twist.** They find a small cavern where all the missing roots have been carefully replanted — upside down, growing into the ceiling of the cave. It's a garden, underground. And tending it is Rosie, a small mole cricket, humming to herself. She's not mean or mischievous — she's lonely. She saw Savannah's beautiful garden from below and wanted one of her own, but didn't know how to grow things without pulling them down.

6. **The resolution.** Savannah doesn't scold Rosie. She sits down with her and asks about her underground garden. Rosie shows her the glowing mushrooms, the crystal-clear water, the soft warm earth. Savannah has an idea: they'll build a garden together — half above, half below. Plants that send roots deep for Rosie and grow tall for Savannah. A garden that grows in both directions at once. Everyone helps: Digby digs connecting tunnels, Pip finds the best spots for seeds, Luna packs the earth flat with her big warm belly, and Rosie plants from below while Savannah plants from above.

7. **Happily ever after.** The new garden grows twice as well as the old one. Every morning, Savannah stands in the sunshine and says, "Good morning, garden. Good morning, sun. Good morning, everyone." And from below the soil, in a soft, happy voice, Rosie answers back: "Good morning, Savannah." And the garden grows — up and down, together.

## Repeated Motifs

- "Good morning, garden. Good morning, sun. Good morning, everyone." — Savannah's morning ritual, used at the opening, when the garden is fading, and at the end with Rosie's reply
- "Deeper and deeper, darker and warmer, closer and closer." — the underground journey refrain, repeated three times as they go further down
